This Mask or That Mask

With the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, the scientist decided to evaluate different face masks based on their speech qualities, breathability, protection against respiratory droplets, and reusability in order to find out the best mask. The masks that were tested were: a single-layer cloth mask, a double-layer cloth mask, a medical face mask, a KN95 mask and an N95 mask.

For speech, a mask was placed in between a speaker and a microphone and the drop-in amplitude (volume) of the sound measured. For breathability, a mask was placed in between an air blower and an anemometer and the speed of the air transmitted was measured. Protection against respiratory droplets was concluded based on how much water was collected after 54 hours when a mask covered a bowl of 30 grams of calcium chloride pellets. Finally, reusability was estimated based on how much bacteria could grow on each mask in an 84-hour incubation period.

Observations suggest that the single-layer cloth mask was best for speech and breathability, the N95 and KN95 were best in terms of protection against respiratory droplets, and the medical face mask was best for reusability.

Overall, after assigning a 10x weight to protection, a 3x weight to breathability, a 2x weight to reusability, and a 1x weight to speech, the N95 was determined to be best, the KN95 to be second best, the single-layer cloth mask to be third best, the double-layer cloth mask to be fourth best and the medical face mask to be the least optimal.

EyeAI: An Artificial Intelligence Assisted Retinal Disease Detection and Physician Support System for Equitable Access to Care

Ocular disease causes visual impairments in many individuals globally, and there is a lack of ophthalmologists to diagnose ocular disease. Furthermore, underprivileged areas often have little to no access to physicians, therefore people in these regions cannot receive a diagnosis of ocular disease to determine the need for further treatment. Machine learning models can diagnose ocular disease based on retinal fundus images and provide a low-cost, low-touch system for ocular disease diagnosis. Artificial intelligence models have demonstrated excellent performance and can be used in clinical practice for enhancing diagnostic performance, treatment quality, and patient outcomes. The system created uses a convoluted neural network (CNN) machine learning model trained on 3200 retinal fundus images for binary classification of diseased or healthy retinas and achieved an accuracy of 80% with an area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (AUROC) value of 0.698. This web-based system, the EyeAI System, can efficiently provide an affordable diagnosis of ocular disease to complement physician decision-making and to serve a wider population.

HealthCheck: Emergency Detection Using Smart Devices

The research focuses on developing and evaluating the HealthCheck system, a voice-activated emergency detection platform aimed at providing rapid response capabilities for individuals, particularly those living alone, elderly people, and those with mobility issues. Motivated by the growing number of single-person households and the heightened risk of health emergencies in elderly individuals, the study leverages advancements in smart device technology to address the challenges of emergency detection and response. The system utilizes a combination of voice detection, text-to-speech, and messaging APIs integrated into a user-friendly application developed on a Raspberry Pi platform. The study hypothesizes that such technology can accurately detect health emergencies and initiate appropriate responses. Through rigorous testing, the HealthCheck system demonstrated reliable performance in recognizing various user commands and appropriately activating emergency protocols. The findings support the potential of smart devices in enhancing emergency response for vulnerable populations, although future improvements could focus on more precise noise monitoring methods. The research concludes that the HealthCheck system significantly contributes to mitigating health and social emergencies, underscoring the transformative potential of smart technology in societal health management.
